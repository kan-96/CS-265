Write a program which reads floats, one per line, stores them in an array. After input, compute and print the mean and standard deviation of the collection. You may assume that there will be no more than 100 inputs.

Call your program sd.c.

See the example readlines.c. The getline/loop reads until EOF, or a blank line (just a newline) is read. You'll also need sscanf .

Remember, the standard deviation is the square root of the average of the squares of the differences between each value and the mean. Given a member of the population, $x$, and the size of the population, $N$:

$$ \sigma_N = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N\left(x_i - \bar{x}\right)^2} $$

(This is an exercise, for practise with several skills, including arrays. Do not compute the standard deviation using the sum of the squares. Also, for simplicity's sake, we will not concern ourselves with Bessel's correction.)
